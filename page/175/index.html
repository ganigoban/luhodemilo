Why did the NHL add the trapezoid?

27 Aug 2024, 00:00

To combat low-scoring and slow hockey, the NHL created the trapezoid in 2004. Legendary New Jersey Devils goaltender Martin Brodeur is often credited with forcing the NHL to implement the trapezoid. Why did the NHL make the trapezoid rule? The trapezoid rule, also known as Rule 1.8 in the NHL rulebook, was introduced after the 2004-05 lockout. Brodeur, a phenomenal puck-handling goaltender, was one of the chief reasons for the introduction of the rule as the NHL tried to cut down on goalies leaving their crease to stop dump-ins in the corners.
